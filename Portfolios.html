Portfolio Rubric

A portfolio is a sample of your past work that highlights your process for delivering high-quality work and shows your ability to work through problems and deliver results.

If you’re ready to start building your portfolio, or if you have a portfolio and are looking to update it, these tips can help. The information presented is based on recent alumni and employer feedback.

UX Portfolio Best Practices

Front Page

  • Keep the page clean, simple, and engaging
  • Feature at least 3 completed projects with clear context
  • Avoid extra-large images to reduce excessive scrolling
  • Highlight “About Me” information such as interests and passions
  • Create a unique and personal tagline
  • Maintain consistent branding (logos, colors, graphics)
  • Use a clear and consistent structure throughout the portfolio

About Me

  • Show your personality and include a professional photo
  • Describe who you are and how you fit into a team
  • Tell your story in 2–3 paragraphs
  • Explain your UX focus and professional journey

Projects

  • Summarize each project: Role, Client, Duration, Challenge
  • Show your full process, not just final results
  • Include artifacts such as wireframes, personas, prototypes, and reports
  • Identify key takeaways and lessons learned
  • Personas are especially valuable for UX Research roles
  • If under NDA, create modified or new artifacts that demonstrate learning without violating agreements

Navigation and Links

  • Include a link to your resume or embed it on a separate page
  • Add icons linking to professional social platforms (LinkedIn, GitHub, etc.)
  • Provide “Next” and “Previous” buttons between projects
  • Include descriptions for all images
  • Consider using Google Analytics to track views

Overall Tips

  • Customize templates to reflect your personal brand
  • Do not label pages as “in progress”
  • Design with usability in mind—avoid unnecessary redirects
  • Avoid clip art and unrelated stock images
  • Use motion and animation sparingly and intentionally
  • Proofread every page carefully
  • Avoid UMSI-specific terms; explain experiences clearly and concisely
