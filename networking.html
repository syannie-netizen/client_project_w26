Networking & Connections Support

Why Networking Matters

  1. Learn the real day-to-day work of industries and roles
  2. Access hidden opportunities such as informational interviews and referrals
  3. Build long-term professional and career support systems

Ways to Build Connections

Step 1: Find Alumni

UMSI has a large and active alumni network across top companies and organizations. Use these CDO resources to search for alumni by industry, role, or company—then reach out for an informational interview or just to connect.

CDO Resources

  • UMSI LinkedIn Group
  • UMSI Alumni Tool
  • CareerLink (Employer Contacts & Events)
  • UCAN (University Career Alumni Network)
  • Alumni Career Connections (via CareerLink)
  • Networking or Recruiting Events (via CareerLink)

Step 2: Build your network

Networking does not only mean reaching out to strangers. Start with the people you already know—classmates, professors, and past employers are all part of your network. Then gradually expand outward using LinkedIn and UMSI events.

  1. Friends and peers (your personal network): Your classmates, study group members, and student organization contacts are often your most accessible connections. They may share job leads, refer you to their employers, or become future colleagues in your field.
  2. Faculty, supervisors, and mentors (your professional network): Professors and past supervisors already know your work ethic and skills firsthand. They can provide strong referrals, introduce you to industry contacts, and offer guidance on career paths within your area of interest.
  3. LinkedIn search beyond your immediate connections: LinkedIn allows you to search for UMSI alumni working at companies you are interested in. Even if you have never met them, a shared school or program is a natural starting point for reaching out and starting a conversation.

How to Network Effectively

(1) Goal: Get a Response

Your primary goal in any outreach is simply to get a response. Do not get discouraged by a low response rate — on average, cold outreach receives about a 20% response rate. Every person who replies is a valuable addition to your professional network.

Common ways to connect:

  1. Phone call — quick and personal, good for warm connections
  2. Zoom or video meeting — convenient for long-distance contacts
  3. In-person meeting — most memorable, best for building rapport
  4. Email — most common method for cold outreach

(2) Writing an Effective Email

A good networking email does four things: it establishes context, states a clear ask, shows genuine interest in the recipient, and makes it easy to say yes.

  • Subject line: Write something specific and engaging — avoid generic lines like "Hello" or "Quick Question"
  • Opening: Start with a shared connection, mutual interest, or how you found them
  • The ask: Be specific about what you want, be concise about why you chose them in particular, and define a clear timeline
  • Closing: End with a sincere thank you and make it easy for them to respond

(3) Writing an Effective LinkedIn Message

LinkedIn messages have a 300-character limit, so every word counts. Follow the same structure as an email but keep it much shorter — one or two sentences is ideal.

  • Context: Briefly explain how or why you found them
  • The ask: State clearly what you are looking for and why you are reaching out to them specifically
  • Timeline: Define a clear and easy timeframe so they can say yes quickly

Pro Tip: If you join the UMSI LinkedIn Group, you can message other members without the 300-character limit.
